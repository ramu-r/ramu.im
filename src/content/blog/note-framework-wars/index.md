[This nx.dev piece](https://nx.dev/blog/meta-harnesses-agents-and-lessons-from-the-framework-wars) credits React's win to doing less than its rivals. I read it the other way. React won because there were fewer pieces to hold in your head, not because the framework was smaller.

With Backbone or EmberJs you had to learn controllers, services, directives. React asked you to learn components and state. You could build Facebook on just useState and useEffect before reaching for anything else. It was the easier mental model that helped React win, and it's against that Anthropic and co are fighting, by making a simple chat as complex as they can.
